History of Coffee Beans 

    History of Coffee Beans :

  9th Century, Ethiopia: Coffee discovered by a goat herder named Kaldi.
  15th Century, Yemen: Cultivation and trade began; used by Sufi monks.
  16th–17th Century, Middle East & Europe: Spread to Persia, Turkey, and Europe; coffee houses became social hubs.
  17th–18th Century, Asia & Americas: Dutch brought it to Indonesia; introduced to the Caribbean and Brazil.
  19th Century: Large-scale commercial coffee production, Brazil becomes top producer.
  20th–21st Century: Instant coffee, espresso culture, specialty coffee, focus on sustainability and fair trade.

Arabica

Scientific name: Coffea arabica
Origin: Native to the highlands of Ethiopia
Growing conditions: Prefers high altitudes, cooler temperatures, and tropical climates; delicate and harder to grow
Bean shape: Oval with a curved crease in the middle
Flavor profile: Smooth, mild, less bitter; often has fruity, nutty, or chocolatey notes
Caffeine content: Lower than Robusta (about 1–1.5%)
Popularity: Makes up 60–70% of global coffee production
Price: More expensive due to delicate growing requirements and superior flavor

Robusta

Scientific name: Coffea canephora (commonly known as Robusta)
Origin: Native to central and western sub-Saharan Africa
Growing conditions: Grows at lower altitudes, tolerates hotter climates; hardier and more resistant to pests and diseases
Bean shape: Smaller and rounder than Arabica, with a straight crease in the middle
Flavor profile: Strong, bold, more bitter; often described as earthy, woody, or grain-like, sometimes with chocolatey notes
Caffeine content: Higher than Arabica (about 2–2.7%)
Popularity: Accounts for about 30–40% of global coffee production
Price: Less expensive than Arabica due to easier cultivation and higher yields

Liberica

Scientific name: Coffea Liberica
Origin: Native to West and Central Africa
Growing conditions: Grows in low to medium altitudes; requires humid, tropical climates; more tolerant of harsh conditions than Arabica but less common than Robusta
Bean shape: Large and irregularly shaped, often asymmetrical with a “hooked” end
Flavor profile: Distinctive, smoky, floral, and fruity; sometimes described as woody or nutty with a full body
Caffeine content: Moderate, between Arabica and Robusta (about 1.2–1.8%)
Popularity: Represents a small fraction of coffee production, often used in blends
Price: Generally more expensive than Robusta due to rarity and lower yield

Excelsa

Scientific name: : Coffea excelsa
Origin: Native to Southeast Asia, mainly the Philippines and surrounding regions
Growing conditions: Grows in low to medium altitudes; thrives in humid, tropical climates; often used in blends to add tart, fruity notes
Bean shape: Medium-sized, often oval and slightly elongated with a somewhat irregular crease
Flavor profile:Tart, fruity, and sometimes wine-like; has a full body with a unique, complex taste that can be both dark and bright
Caffeine content: Moderate, similar to Arabica (about 1.5–1.8%)
Popularity: Rare globally; mostly used in specialty blends or for unique flavor profiles
Price: Generally more expensive than Robusta due to rarity and limited cultivation
